My quest to find Gulab Jamun
in New York City

By Pooja Chaudhuri

Feb 4, 2023



I love Gulab Jamun. For those unaware (and missing out so much in life), Gulab Jamun is a South Asian dessert traditionaly made out of khoya or dried milk. It can also be prepared from flour but the traditional method is, in my opinion, a lot better. If I had to oversimplyfy - Gulab Jamun is like a donut dipped in sugar syrup.

I haven't had Gulab Jamun since I moved to New York City last August. And I wasn't really missing it until we were given a prompt in our class - "What is it that you miss from home?"

This project is my quest to find Gulab Jamun in NYC.





I found there aren't many restaurants in Upper Manhattan (where I live) that sell Gulab Jamun. So if I have to travel to Lower Manhattan, I should know what I'm getting into.

Gulab Jamuns are expensive in NYC. Most restaurants sell two pieces for $6 to $8. For those that did not mention the quantity, I assumed it was two pieces. (Methodological choice!! )




By now, I am quite disappointed. I used to have two pieces for not more than 60 cents in India. I wondered if there was an alternative. Can I buy canned gulab jamuns on Amazon?

It turns out that I can but there are not a lot of options. My pick is MTR because it is the cheapest option on Amazon. And it is rated 4 out of 5 by at least 90 users. But Bikano, on the other hand, though much more expensive, has the same rating but by a lot more people - over 5000. This doesn't help a lot!




Since I am already sad and disappointed, I thought I'll find out if my least favorite dessert is comparitively more popular in NYC. That would be the last straw!

I really dislike matcha cheesecake. I've had it once in my life and it was the worst. I hate it even more because I love cheesecake and matcha ruins it for me.

But to my delight, it isn't as popular as I'd imagined. Since cheesecake is so popular in NYC, I thought matcha cheesecake would be too. But that doesn't seem to be the case.




I have spent more time on this that I would have liked to but something good has come out of this quest.

I found a restaurant that sells GULAB JAMUN CHEESECAKES!! We finally have a winner and for those who made it till here, the restaurant is linked here.


Methodology:

I used Yelp API to get the data on restaurants. The project may not be an accurate representation since I only relied on one source and there could also be several other restaurants that are not listed online. I used Datawrapper for the interactive visualizations and pandas for the static bar chart. All code for the project can be found on my GitHub.
