Roosters keep NYC 311 busy with "illegal pet" complaints

By Pooja C

February 19, 2023


While New York City is known for its love for tiny dogs - that fit inside tote bags and prams - there are a few people who have taken an unconventional appraoch to pet ownership.

Roosters!

According to Open Data NYC, complaints about roosters have kept the city's 311 hotline busy. Since 2010, NYC 311 has received 1994 complaints about roosters kept as illegal pets mostly in residences.

Other animals that keep the complaints going are farm animals, 599 complaints, and snakes, 278 complaints.

While the data does not specify farm animals that caused people to dial 311 and complain, the webiste of NYC 311 mentions that "sheep, goats, pigs, and most farm animals" are not allowed to be kept as pets.

Ferret, monkey, iguana and turtle (under four inches) were the other aniamls that brought complaints.

However, complaints about roosters seem to be the most worrying since there have been past reports of the animal attacking humans.

In 2021, NBC reported that a rooster attack left a man in Queens, on his way to work, bloodied. The incident occurred in 169th Street in Jamaica.

"It's hard not to chuckle at the idea that people now have to keep an eye out for an attacking rooster on New York City streets. But anyone who lives in the area says it's no joke," NBC wrote at the time.

The data shows that most complaints are from Queens.

Last year, there were 183 complaints from Queens about illegal pets, followed by 94 complaints from Bronx and 81 complaints from Brooklyn.
